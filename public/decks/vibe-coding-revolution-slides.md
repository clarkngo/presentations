## Welcome Aboard!

Imagine you wanted to build a house — but instead of laying bricks yourself, you had a magical builder who did exactly what you said, instantly.

<p><strong>That is Vibe Coding.</strong></p>

<p>We're moving from <em>writing code</em> (a foreign language) to <em>sharing ideas</em> (just talking).</p>

> Building real, working apps by describing them in plain English. <!-- .element: -->

Note:
The house metaphor is the hook — give it a beat before defining the term.

--

## Why This Is a Game-Changer

<p><strong>No coding experience required.</strong> You don't need to know what a variable, a database, or a semicolon is.</p>

<p><strong>Instant superpowers.</strong> A custom calculator, a travel planner, a study tool — build and use it <em>today</em>, not next year.</p>

<p><strong>The ultimate leverage.</strong> You focus 100% on <em>what</em> makes your app cool and useful. The AI worries about <em>how</em> to make the buttons click.</p>

## Module 2 — A Quick History

How did we get this superpower?

--

## Talking to Computers Used to Be Hard

<div class="phase">
<strong>Phase 1 — The Stone Age</strong>
Humans had to talk like computers. Punch cards. Raw 1s and 0s. One tiny typo broke everything.
</div>

<div class="phase">
<strong>Phase 2 — The Code Era</strong>
Programming languages (Python, JavaScript). Easier — but months learning exact grammar and syntax.
</div>

<div class="phase vibe">
<strong>Phase 3 — The Vibe Era</strong>
<strong>Computers learned to talk like humans.</strong> Now you use your normal, everyday voice.
</div>

Note:
Walk these three phases like a timeline. Pause on Phase 3 — that's the room's "aha."

--

## How the "Vibe" Became Official

<p><strong>The spark:</strong> In early 2025, AI scientist Andrej Karpathy noticed people building amazing apps without looking at a single line of code.</p>

<p>He called it <strong>"Vibe Coding"</strong> — fully leaning into the creative flow and letting the AI handle the gears under the hood.</p>

<p><strong>Today:</strong> Creators, artists, teachers, and students build software every day — without being "programmers."</p>

## Module 3 — The Secret Loop

How it actually works.

--

## The Captain & Copilot Loop

Vibe coding is a conversation. You don't prompt once and walk away — you work together:

<div class="loop-path">
<span>Describe</span>
<span class="arrow">→</span>
<span>Test</span>
<span class="arrow">→</span>
<span>Refine</span>
<span class="arrow">→</span>
<span>Repeat</span>
</div>

1. **Describe (The Spark)** — *"Make me a simple flight fuel calculator with a clean blue theme."* <!-- .element: -->
2. **Test (The Takeoff)** — Run it. Click everything. What works? What feels off? <!-- .element: -->
3. **Refine (The Adjustment)** — *"Make the button turn green when clicked, and add a warning if the fuel is too low."* <!-- .element: -->
4. **Repeat** — Watch it get better with every exchange. <!-- .element: -->

Note:
Say the loop out loud once. This is the only mechanical skill they're learning.

--

## Golden Rules for Non-Tech Captains

<div class="phase">
<strong>Be specific.</strong>
Not <em>"make a cool app"</em> — <em>"a daily planner with 3 checkable boxes and a funny quote at the bottom."</em>
</div>

<div class="phase">
<strong>Take small steps.</strong>
Don't ask for a social network in one go. One page. Then a button. Then a feature.
</div>

<div class="phase vibe">
<strong>Bugs are normal.</strong>
Don't panic. Tell the Copilot: <em>"The red button doesn't work when I click it. Fix it please."</em> It will correct itself.
</div>

Note:
Emphasize "bugs are normal" — first-timers freeze when something breaks. Normalize the fix loop.
